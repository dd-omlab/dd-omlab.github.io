OMLAB EYE-MOVEMENT DATA: Scientific Publications

The following are deidentified, downloadable copies of human subjects' digitized eye-movement data from papers published in refereed scientific journals. The criteria required for these data are: 1) all the data were collected in OMLAB; 2) our calibrated eye-movement systems were used; 3) our nystagmus paradigms were followed; and 4) the patient's privacy would not be compromised by release of the data. It is hoped that this will be the start of a usable eye-movement database that researchers can access to conduct future studies. The data are accurate, monocularly calibrated, and include monocular and binocular fixation, smooth pursuit, and vergence responses from subjects with INS, FMNS, NBS, SPNS, as well as different types of acquired nystagmus and saccadic oscillations. Depending on the type of data (e.g., ir, magnetic search coil, or high-speed digital video), horizontal, vertical, and torsional eye-movement channels plus head-movement channels may be available. Horizontal and vertical stimulus channels may also be included.

To locate eye-mvement data from the subects studied in a specific paper, search the page for the paper # and title. Then download the zipped data file corresponding to that paper.

To understand how the data were generated, how to use OMtools to retrieve data files into MATLAB (using 'rd'), how to plot the results (using various plot programs) and how to use the NAFX to analyze nystagmus data, it is recommended that you first download and read OMLAB Reports #011105 and #111005 from the Reports & Teaching page and then the OMLAB & NIH Data Handling file.
